Companies that rely heavily on oil face substantial uncertainty because oil prices are highly volatile and can change rapidly in response to global events. 
Factors such as supply chain disruptions, economic sanctions, wars, OPEC decisions, and shipping route issues all impact oil prices.
The Brent Oil month-year table (from January 1946 to March 2026) provides clear evidence of these dramatic price swings over time, emphasizing the ongoing risk.Business Impact:

Industries affected: Logistics, airlines, manufacturing, food delivery, and any company dependent on imports are especially vulnerable.

Consequences of price spikes: A sudden increase in oil prices can cause fuel, shipping, production, and supply costs to rise sharply, which erodes profit margins and can threaten business viability.


Risk Management Strategies:
Monitor Brent oil prices monthly to stay aware of trends and anticipate changes.
Use flexible pricing in contracts to adjust for cost increases and avoid locked-in losses.
Set aside emergency funds as a financial buffer for unexpected spikes in oil-related costs.
Diversify suppliers and shipping routes to reduce vulnerability to disruptions in any one region or route.
Consider hedging or locking in fixed fuel prices through financial contracts, especially when the global outlook is uncertain.
By proactively managing these risks, companies can better protect their profit margins and maintain operational stability even in volatile oil markets.
